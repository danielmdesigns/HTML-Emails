What is a Commission Advance?
A commission advance is a financial service whereby an agent and broker sell a portion of a future real estate commission for a fee. In exchange, funds are advanced before closing. It’s not a loan, rather, access to an earned commission prior to an estimated settlement date.

Why Does the Broker Sign the Advance Agreement?
As broker, all commissions earned by your agents belong to you. We cannot advance funds without your consent. The agreement you sign authorizes the assignment of the agent’s net commission on a particular sale, after any split with you, to eCommission.

Does the Broker Have Any Risk or Liability?
No. In the unlikely event we advance a commission to one of your agents on a sale that falls through, it is the agent’s responsibility to replace it using future commissions.
